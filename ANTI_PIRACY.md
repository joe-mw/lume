# Anti-Piracy Policy

Lume is an open-source **media player**. This document explains our position on
piracy, what Lume is and is not, and the rules we expect everyone in our community
to follow.

**TL;DR — Lume is a player, not a content provider. It ships with no channels,
streams, playlists, or media of any kind. You are responsible for only connecting it
to content you are legally entitled to access.**

---

## Our position

We unequivocally **do not condone or support piracy** in any form.

Lume exists to give people a clean, modern, native way to watch media they already
have the right to watch — a legitimate IPTV subscription, their own M3U/M3U8
playlists, free-to-air and public streams, or their personal media library. It does
**not** exist to access, distribute, or facilitate access to content that you do not
have the right to use.

Building a player is legal. Using it to consume pirated or unauthorized content is
not — and that line is yours to respect.

---

## What Lume is (and is not)

| Lume **is** | Lume **is not** |
|---|---|
| A native player UI for Apple platforms | A source of channels, streams, or media |
| A client for **your** Xtream Codes / M3U credentials | An IPTV subscription or content provider |
| Open-source software you can read, build, and modify | A service that resells or rebroadcasts content |
| A tool that enriches metadata via public APIs (TMDB, OMDb, Trakt) | A directory or aggregator of streams |

Lume **bundles no content and pre-configures no providers.** Every channel, movie,
or series you see comes exclusively from credentials or playlists **you** supply.

---

## Permitted use

Lume is intended to be used with content you are **legally entitled** to access,
including:

- **Paid IPTV subscriptions** from a provider you are properly licensed to use, where
  the provider itself holds the rights to the content it distributes.
- **Your own M3U / M3U8 playlists**, including local files and URLs you control.
- **Free-to-air, public, and openly licensed** streams (e.g. public broadcaster
  feeds, community channels, and other content offered freely and lawfully).
- **Your personal media** served from your own infrastructure.

---

## Prohibited conduct

In Lume's repository, issues, pull requests, Discord, and any other community space,
the following is **not allowed**:

- **Requesting, sharing, selling, or advertising** IPTV subscriptions, playlists,
  Xtream credentials, or streams that distribute copyrighted content without
  authorization.
- **Linking to** piracy services, "free IPTV" lists, credential dumps, or any source
  whose purpose is to circumvent paying rights holders.
- **Asking for help** configuring Lume specifically to access pirated or unauthorized
  content.
- Using Lume's name, code, or builds to **promote or enable** piracy.

Violations may result in **comment/post removal, issue or PR closure, bans from the
community**, and — where appropriate — reporting to the relevant platform.

Please keep all support requests, bug reports, and discussions focused on the
**software itself**, not on where to obtain content.

---

## Why this matters

Piracy undermines the people who create, license, and distribute content, and it puts
the legal future of open, user-respecting media players at risk. Keeping Lume strictly
a **player** — and keeping our community free of piracy — protects the project, its
contributors, and everyone who relies on it for legitimate use.

We would rather be a great tool for people who do the right thing than a convenient one
for people who don't.

## Copyright & takedowns

Lume's own source code is licensed under the **AGPL-3.0** (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)).
It contains no third-party copyrighted media.

If you believe content or links posted **within our community spaces** (issues, pull
requests, discussions) infringe your rights, please open an issue or contact the
maintainers so it can be removed promptly. Note that Lume cannot remove content from
third-party providers or playlists — those are operated by parties entirely outside
this project.

## Your responsibility

By using Lume you agree that:

- You are solely responsible for the content you choose to access through it.
- You will only connect it to providers and playlists you are **legally entitled** to use.
- The Lume project and its contributors are **not responsible** for how you use the
  software, and provide it "as is" without warranty (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)).

If you are unsure whether your use is legitimate, the safest assumption is: **if you
are not paying a rights holder or accessing content offered freely and lawfully, you
probably should not be accessing it.**
